Discover Fernando de Noronha

officially the State District of Fernando de Noronha (Portuguese: Distrito Estadual de Fernando de Noronha) and formerly known as the Territory of Fernando de Noronha (Portuguese: Territ√≥rio de Fernando de Noronha) until 1988, is an archipelago in the Atlantic Ocean, part of the State of Pernambuco, Brazil, and located 354 km (191 nmi; 220 mi) off the Brazilian coast. It consists of 21 islands and islets, extending over an area of 26 km2 (10 sq mi).[2] Only the eponymous main island is inhabited; it has an area of 18.4 km2 (7.1 sq mi) and a population estimated at 3,101 in 2020. While most of the archipelago is relatively low-lying, there are parts reaching more than 100 m (328 ft) in elevation.

What Citizens Say

The moment you arrive on the island and catch your first glimpse of its crystal-clear waters, dramatic cliffs, and vibrant marine life, you'll likely be awestruck by the sheer beauty of the place. Fernando de Noronha is a remote and tranquil destination, making it an ideal place to unwind and relax. The slow pace of life, lack of urban hustle and bustle, and the gentle sound of the ocean can create a sense of peace and relaxation.
